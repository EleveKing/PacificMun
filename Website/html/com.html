Committees

The Committees team has worked diligently to create a committees program that offers a true global perspective that is unique, educational, and exhilarating. Our diverse additions to the conference reflect our unwavering commitment to developing international consciousness at the high school level.

For this year's session of CAHSMUN, we are especially committed to building knowledge of Canadian issues, through our Canadian-themed committees. Too often, we overlook Canada's political structure and the capabilities of Canada on the international field, which was our main motivation to increase Canadian perspective at CAHSMUN. The Canadian House of Commons (HOC) and the Canadian Security Intelligence Service (CSIS) will undoubtedly provide a compelling weekend of high-quality and lively Canada-centric discussion and debate.

With our introduction of the Berlin Conference of 1884 (BC84), we strive to bring an entirely new challenge to the histrorical committee. Delegates will be sent back to the nineteenth century to ultimately decide the fate of the control of Africa. Delegates will be placed in the hot seat of diplomacy, and must rely on their understanding of the state of foreign affairs during that time and their diplomatic abilities in order to secure a victory for their country.

Rules of Procedure Guide

PPP

The requirement for position papers varies between each committee, based on the difficulty level of the committee. Each committee page indicates whether or not position papers are mandatory. However, position papers are always mandatory in order to be considered for a committee award.

The position paper deadline for all committees at CAHSMUN 2016 is March 8, 2016. In the case of extenuating circumstances, delegates may request an extension by contacting committee directors through the committee email. Extensions will be granted at the discretion of the director in question. Directors will not be able to give extensions beyond March 10, 2016 at 11:59 PM.
